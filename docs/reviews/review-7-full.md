## Executive Summary — the ten things that matter

1. **Your gate is the bug, not your candidates. Promote now and re-spec the gate.** A fixed SPRT with elo0=0/elo1=30 across a run whose true per-turn gains are shrinking is a known anti-pattern: fishtest's bounds *shrink as the engine matures* (production Stockfish patches test at roughly {0, 2} Elo bounds today) **[LIT]**; KataGo's author calls the gatekeeper optional, "nice when starting out a run to help debugging," and never ran a controlled gated-vs-ungated test **[LIT]**; AlphaZero and MuZero ran fully ungated **[LIT]**. Your pooled 52.8%/1000-games candidate is real (+~20 Elo). Every day you withhold promotion you withhold the only thing expert iteration compounds on — data generated by the better policy. Switch to regression-protection gating (elo0=−10/elo1=+15) with the external panel as the binding tripwire, and promote the current candidate. Details §3.

2. **There is a structural fix for your value-head fragility, and it's cheap: categorical value with cross-entropy (HL-Gauss).** Farebrother et al., "Stop Regressing" (ICML 2024 Oral, arXiv:2403.03950) show MSE value regression "results in performance degradation with prolonged training. However, cross-entropy losses (both HL-Gauss and C51) do not show such degradation and generally remain stable" — a near-exact description of your four failures **[LIT]**. You already *built* a categorical head. This is your single highest-expected-value training experiment. §4.1.

3. **Your λ=0.5 value-target blend bootstraps the value head on its own outputs — a self-distillation amplifier you've already been burned by.** The search-root-value half of the target comes from the *same net that generated the game*. EfficientZero-v2's Search-Based Value Estimation (ICML 2024) and MuZero-Reanalyse show the bootstrap component should be recomputed by the *current/lagged* net, not read from the archived root value **[LIT]**. This plausibly contributed to your +69% continuous-lineage drift. Reanalyze-lite (recompute value targets on the stored window with the champion) is both the fix and a plateau-breaker. §4.2.

4. **Your plateau diagnosis is partially instrument error.** The anchor holdout is pinned to one gen-3-era fleet wave. A flat anchor across nine rounds cannot distinguish "the window is fully distilled" from "the anchor is saturated / drifting off-distribution as the policy improves." Refresh anchors every generation (keep the old ones as a series) before concluding anything from flatness **[JUDGMENT]**. §2.4.

5. **The biggest untapped strength lever is raising the quality of the expert, not the trainer.** Per-turn gain in expert iteration is bounded by the search's edge over its own prior. You made simulations 13–19× cheaper (lazy chance), the fleet ~3× faster (MPS), and featurization ~25× faster (Rust) — and left n_full at 64 and never turned on the 3.3× value-denoise from symmetry averaging you already built. KataGo ships exactly this (`rootNumSymmetriesToSample`) **[LIT]**; MiniZero shows raising sims once the net is strong produces a +276-Elo jump at the crossover **[LIT]**. Raise n_full to 128–256 for generation, enable root symmetry averaging at wide roots. §5, experiment #4.

6. **Wire what you've already built.** Opponent pool (built, unwired), Go-Exploit restarts (built, unwired), categorical head (built, untested), D1 noise-floor (mild winner in your own ablation, dormant), symmetry averaging (built, off). Your build-to-deploy ratio is a systematic leak. The spinning-top result (Czarnecki et al., NeurIPS 2020) gives the principled reason population play is not optional at middling skill: cyclic strategy density is *largest in the middle of the skill range* — exactly where you are **[LIT]**. §5.

7. **Train against the target.** Your stated goal is beating a specific fixed bot. Nothing in the AZ framework forbids putting games *versus catanatron_value* (search still generates the targets) into the training mix at 10–20%. Poker exploiter work (AlphaExploitem 2026; L2E) shows targeted-opponent training without general regression **[LIT]**; no board-game AZ project has done it — an open combination you can take **[LIT: gap verified]**. §5, experiment #5.

8. **Several of your headline scientific claims outrun their statistics.** The "λ=0.5 is the most validated training-science result" rests on 59.0% vs 57.0% across two 400-game gates — not a significant difference, and it's the winner of a 7-arm matrix (winner's curse). The "compression trend" (+49→+49→+33→+20) has overlapping CIs turn-to-turn. Neither claim survives audit at the confidence you state it. Both are cheap to actually establish. §2.1–2.2.

9. **Citation and novelty hygiene before you show this to anyone else.** The "Charlesworth PPO Catan paper" doesn't exist as an arXiv paper (his arXiv paper is Big 2, arXiv:1808.10442; the Catan work is a blog + repo, "not quite at the level of a good human"). "CatAnalysis" is unfindable under that name. And preempt HexMachina (OpenReview 2026): an LLM-code-evolution agent claiming 54.1% vs catanatron AlphaBeta — gray literature, not RL, but a sharp reviewer will find it, so qualify "nothing beats catanatron" to "no peer-reviewed learning-based agent" **[LIT]**. §6.4.

10. **Your two novelty claims mostly hold, with one framing correction.** The Gumbel+chance-node gap is confirmed by DeepMind's own maintainer declining to build it (mctx issue #66) **[LIT]**. The rescale-noise mechanism appears genuinely undiagnosed anywhere (closest: mctx issue #108, same symptom class, open and unanswered) **[LIT]**. But note: mctx's default c_scale=0.1 is the *Atari* constant from the paper; the paper's board-game value is 1.0. Your 0.03 is 3× below the shipped default and 33× below the board-game setting — the paper itself already established 10× domain-dependence, so frame your finding as *extending* a known sensitivity to a newly-characterized failure regime (wide, near-tied, stochastic, sub-1-sim/action), not contradicting the paper. §6.1.

## 1. Overall assessment

This is one of the most disciplined small-team AZ efforts I've reviewed. The things you do well are genuinely rare: paired pentanomial gates, fail-closed regime guards, a kill list you refuse to refund, controlled both-regime leak forensics, and negative results reported at the same resolution as positive ones. Three specific consequences: a gate spec that can no longer pass real improvements (§3), a value-target scheme with a self-amplifying feedback path (§4.2), and a pile of built-but-unwired diversity/denoising machinery while the loop inbreeds on single-lineage self-play (§5). ## 2. What you're doing wrong (audit findings)

### 2.1 Winner's curse in the recipe matrix — the λ=0.5 claim is overclaimed
You gated 7 arms vs gen-1 at ~400 games each, then declared the best (H1, 59.0%, LLR 6.12) "the strongest gate in project history" and locked λ=0.5 into production. Two problems:
- 59.0% vs 57.0% (H1 vs gen2A control) is a 2-point difference between two independent 400-game estimates, each with ~±5% CI. Not significant. The max of 7 noisy draws is biased upward by construction.
- You never ran the direct H2H: H1-net vs gen2A-net. That's one ~34-minute gate.

The λ direction is *probably* right — soft-Z/blended targets beat pure z in Willemsen et al. (NCA 2022) and in independent practitioner work (Abrams 2018, z/q averaging or annealing beats either alone) **[LIT]** — but your *evidence* for it is weaker than your prose. **[EXPERIMENT]**: run the direct gate; better, re-derive the λ conclusion under the categorical head, since the two interact (§4.1).

### 2.2 The "compression trend" is not yet a measured phenomenon
+49, +49, +33, +20 Elo per turn: turn-1's CI is [+15, +84] Elo (your own [52.1, 61.9]%); turn-3's is roughly [+7, +59]. These overlap almost entirely. A flat +35/turn is consistent with your data. Diminishing returns is *expected* from every AZ run ever published, so the prior is with you — but don't build strategy (§16 Q3) on a trend your gates can't resolve. The cheap fix is the WHR fit (§3.3), which pools every game you've ever played into one trajectory with honest error bars **[JUDGMENT]**.

### 2.3 The pentanomial rationale is stated backwards (methodology is still right)
You justify pentanomial with "binomial SPRT on individual games overstates significance." Fishtest's own measurement (issue #348) found within-pair correlation ≈ −0.15: *negative* correlation, meaning pentanomial variance is ~15% *smaller* than naive independence — correct pairing *increases* power; the naive binomial is conservative, not anticonservative **[LIT]**. Whether your Catan pairs are net-negative (board luck flips with color) or net-positive (one net better on a board type) is an empirical question — your observed split rates (0.4–0.6, vs ~0.49 under independence) suggest weak correlation either way. Keep pentanomial; fix the stated rationale; measure your empirical pair correlation once and put the number in the doc **[EXPERIMENT, ~zero cost]**.

### 2.4 The plateau inference rests on a stale instrument
"Window fully distilled" is inferred from nine rounds of flat anchor telemetry — but the anchor is one pinned gen-3-era wave. As candidates improve past gen-3, the anchor measures fit to an increasingly *off-policy* distribution; flatness there is compatible with genuine on-distribution improvement. Your own external panel (41.0% vs 45.7%, overlapping CIs at n=200) can't adjudicate either. Before concluding "the same data does not contain a bigger candidate": (a) refresh the anchor from the current window's held-out seeds each generation, keeping the old anchors as a longitudinal series; (b) finish the 1000-game panel. **[JUDGMENT + EXPERIMENT]**

### 2.5 Build-and-shelve is a systematic pattern
Counted from your own report: opponent pool (built, unwired), Go-Exploit restart generation (built, unwired), categorical value head (built, untested), test-time symmetry averaging (built, off in production), D1 noise-floor (mild winner, dormant), cross-attention architecture (built, benched — fine, that one lost its A/B). Some shelving is correct discipline. But the first four all attack your two *named* top problems (value noise, self-play narrowing) and none is deployed. The bottleneck is not ideas or code; it's wiring decisions. **[JUDGMENT]**

### 2.6 Smaller items
- **External Elo framing:** "−30 Elo from #1" is bot-relative. No human calibration of catanatron exists anywhere **[LIT: verified absence]**. Don't let "#1 Catan bot" drift into implying human-relative strength claims; the real human-rated world is colonist.io (which also uses a *non-uniform balanced dice* variant — cross-benchmark comparisons need care) **[LIT]**.
- **Scope framing:** 2p-no-trade is deliberately *smaller* than the existing 4p-negotiation DRL literature (Cuayáhuitl et al. 2015/2017: DQN negotiation module, ~82% vs humans on the negotiation subtask) **[LIT]**. Frame it that way before someone frames it for you.
- **Temperature schedule:** T=1.0 for 90 decisions then argmax over ~205-decision games means the back half of every self-play game is deterministic given the state. Gumbel root sampling adds some diversity, but your late-game data diversity rests entirely on board/dice variation. KataGo keeps stochasticity late via cap randomization *and* other noise. Cheap to test: small nonzero temperature (or Gumbel sampling) to decision ~150 **[EXPERIMENT]**.

## 3. Decision 1: the promotion criterion (§12/§16-Q1)

### 3.1 What the precedents actually say
- **AlphaZero** (Silver et al., Science 2018, Methods): "AlphaZero simply maintains a single neural network that is updated continually... Self-play games are always generated by using the latest parameters." No gate at all **[LIT]**.
- **MuZero** (arXiv:1911.08265): latest checkpoint every 1000 steps, no gate **[LIT]**.
- **KataGo**: gatekeeper explicitly optional; docs: runs "often are more efficient without a gatekeeper at all, although a gatekeeper can be nice when starting out a run." Wu chose 50% (not 55%) gating and admits he never controlled-tested gate vs no-gate **[LIT]**.
- **Leela Zero side:** community analysis (LZ issue #1524) simulated expected Elo/net vs threshold: 55% gate → ~7.6 Elo/net; 50% → ~12.0. The *stricter* gate produced *slower* progress — the gate's opportunity cost dominates its protection value once candidates are mostly-good. Marked forum-grade, but the mechanism is exactly your situation **[LIT/forum]**.
- **fishtest**: bounds shrink with maturity; today's Stockfish patches gate at ~{0, 2} logistic Elo **[LIT]**. Nobody holds a maturing system to a fixed +30 bar.

Your own literature synthesis ("small teams kept gates, big teams dropped them") is right as far as it goes, but you drew the wrong operational conclusion from it: KataGo's gate is *regression protection at ~50%*, not an improvement-certification bar at +30. You built a certification instrument and are using it as a promotion valve.

### 3.2 Recommendation **[JUDGMENT, high confidence]**
Adopt option (1) from your §12.4, with specifics:
- **Flywheel gate:** SPRT elo0=−10, elo1=+15, α=β=0.05, 300 games n=16, extensions as now. This passes a true +20 candidate quickly and still blocks true regressions hard.
- **Binding tripwire = external panel, not the internal gate:** promote, then run the 200-game panel; two consecutive promoted champions with declining catanatron_value results → automatic revert to the last externally-confirmed champion and post-mortem. This is DeepNash's validation pattern (fixed external panel + humans, not self-play Elo) **[LIT]**.
- **Promote the current turn-4 candidate now.** Pooled 52.8%/1000 (p≈0.04, and pairing likely makes it stronger) plus the historical-signature anchor telemetry is enough under the new spec. The counterargument (external flatness) is a 200-game instrument that cannot see +20; don't let an underpowered instrument veto a powered one.
- **Keep the +30-style certification gate for *turn* announcements** (discrete "gen-N" claims), decoupled from the flywheel's data-refresh promotions. Two instruments, two jobs.

### 3.3 Add WHR over the whole ladder (novel, cheap, high information)
Whole-History Rating (Coulom 2008) jointly fits time-varying strengths from *all* games ever played — every gate, extension, ablation arm, and panel game you have on disk — with proper uncertainty, instead of starting each 400-game gate from zero **[LIT]**. No AZ-lineage project has adopted WHR/BayesElo for promotion tracking (verified gap) **[LIT]**. For you it: resolves the compression-trend question (§2.2), gives a running champion-trajectory with error bars, and detects a persistent +20 drift across gates that individually say "continue." Pure statistics, zero GPU. Do this regardless of the gate decision. **[EXPERIMENT: 1–2 days of engineering, no fleet time]**

## 4. Decision 2: the value head and the plateau (§16-Q2, Q5)

### 4.1 Categorical value with cross-entropy — the structural fix you asked for
**[LIT, highest confidence recommendation in this review]** Farebrother et al., "Stop Regressing: Training Value Functions via Classification for Scalable Deep RL" (ICML 2024 Oral, arXiv:2403.03950): replacing MSE with categorical cross-entropy over a discretized support (HL-Gauss — Gaussian-smeared histogram targets) yields large gains across domains (chess-without-search +70% over the Ruoss et al. baseline; 1.8–2.1× multi-task Atari), and — the part aimed at you — controlled diagnostics show cross-entropy specifically resists *noisy targets* and *non-stationarity*, with MSE degrading under prolonged training while HL-Gauss/C51 remain stable. Your four failures (multi-epoch blowup 0.665→0.842; 91M epoch-2 blowup; +69% lineage drift; BC value optimum uselessness) are all MSE-under-revisited-distribution failures. HL-Gauss beats plain two-hot in their ablations (two-hot doesn't smear mass to neighbors), so prefer HL-Gauss over your built two-hot variant if they differ. LightZero already ships `categorical_distribution=True` citing this exact paper — this is now community default, not exotica **[LIT]**.

Test protocol **[EXPERIMENT]**: (a) train champion-init on the current window with HL-Gauss head, gate vs champion — expect ≥parity; (b) the *real* test: re-run your 3-epoch arm. If val value loss no longer blows up across epochs, you've unlocked sample reuse >1 — which at your generation-bound economics is equivalent to multiplying the fleet. That's the result that changes your world, not the parity gate.

Complement (cheap, production-proven): KataGo's short-horizon auxiliary TD value heads (~6/16/50-turn horizons) plus its score-distribution analog (you have final-VP aux at 0.1 already — add the TD-horizon heads). Wu's ablation: removing aux ownership/score targets costs 190 Elo / 1.65× training speed — the largest single component in the paper **[LIT]**. Root cause named there matches your law: the value target gets one independent sample per *game* vs the policy's one per *move*; aux targets are how KataGo un-starves it.

### 4.2 Stop feeding the value head its own root values
Your λ=0.5 blend stores the *generating net's* root value in the shard and trains the next net toward it. In discrete one-dose mode this is damped; in the continuous loop it is a textbook self-amplification path (each round's value bias becomes half of the next round's target), and it plausibly co-caused the +69% drift alongside the re-splitting leak. The literature's answer is consistent: recompute the search/bootstrap component with the *current or lagged* net at training time —
- MuZero-Reanalyse (NeurIPS 2021): re-running MCTS with the latest network on stored trajectories is the single largest contributor in their ablation (value-score 92→240 combined) **[LIT]**;
- EfficientZero-v2 (ICML 2024): Search-Based Value Estimation — fresh search with the current model on stale transitions beats multi-step TD and double-Q alternatives **[LIT]**;
- ReZero (arXiv:2404.16364): periodic whole-buffer reanalyze, explicitly framed as the target-network mechanism transplanted to MCTS-RL **[LIT]**.

Concrete version for you **[EXPERIMENT]**: value-only reanalyze-lite — batch-forward the current champion over the stored window's states (no tree needed for a first cut; your lr≈0 probe infrastructure is 80% of this), overwrite the λ-blend's v-component, retrain. Hypothesis: the "fully distilled" window contains another candidate when its value targets are refreshed. This is the plateau-breaker that costs zero new games. If the value-only version moves the anchor, graduate to root-search reanalyze (n=16 fresh searches over stored states, embarrassingly parallel on the A100 fleet between waves).

### 4.3 Value-target down-weighting by uncertainty (optional, later)
Epistemic MCTS (ICLR 2025) and Uncertainty-Guided AlphaZero (NeurIPS 2025 — branches variations from high-uncertainty positions and *ensembles the value labels*, 47–58% sample-efficiency gains on Go) both support uncertainty-weighted value losses/labels **[LIT]**. Your Go-Exploit tooling already reconstructs states bit-exactly — the NeurIPS-2025 recipe (branch from high-uncertainty roots, ensemble outcomes) is nearly wired for you. Second wave, after 4.1/4.2.

## 6. What others did — literature synthesis vs your choices

### 6.3 Imperfect information (relevant to how far masking carries you)

## 8. The most important things you don't know you don't know

1. **You have no defined finish line.** "Beat catanatron_value" is measurable; "#1 Catan bot / superhuman" is not, because *no human calibration of any Catan bot exists* **[LIT: verified absence]**. The only human-rated Catan ecosystem is colonist.io (different dice distribution, 4p-centric, has its own GNN+MCTS bot that self-admits to using full information). Decide now what "won" means — likely: (a) SPRT-certified vs every catanatron bot at 2p no-trade, then (b) a colonist.io-rules variant and human trials. Otherwise you will optimize a bot-relative Elo forever.
2. **The 2p-no-trade moat is also a ceiling.** The moment you claim #1-Catan-bot publicly, the 4p+trading literature (negotiation DQNs beating humans at the trade subtask) and colonist.io's ecosystem become the goalposts. Your engine/featurization choices (2-player token layout, no trade actions) should be audited *now* for how expensive the 4p+trade extension is, before more infrastructure calcifies around 2p **[JUDGMENT]**.
3. **Your policy head may be quietly data-starved.** 7.7% of rows carry policy targets; KataGo's playout-cap randomization was designed around value starvation, but your split (n_full=64 @ 25%) at your new cheap-search economics is probably no longer the right point. Raising p_full and n_full moves both target quality and target quantity — cheap ablation, nobody's asking about it.
4. **Nobody has computed Catan's disambiguation factor** (Long et al. 2010). One afternoon with your replay tooling turns "we believe masking suffices" from assertion into a measured, publishable claim — and tells you *in advance* whether the MAPLE-style multi-world search (+136–291 Elo in strongly-hidden games) is worth anything here **[LIT + EXPERIMENT]**.
5. **Your gates all run at n=8/16 while production is n=64.** You checked gen-1 at both; keep checking every promotion at n=64 periodically — a promotion rule tuned entirely at low sims can drift toward nets that are good priors for weak search and mediocre priors for strong search.
6. **Checkpoint diversity is an asset you're discarding.** The recipe-matrix losers, v3a/v3b, and every past champion are a free style population for pool play and Nash-averaged evaluation. Single-lineage systems pay for diversity later; yours is sitting in a directory.
