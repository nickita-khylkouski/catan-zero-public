## Executive Summary — the ten things that matter most

1. **Your promotion gate is mis-specified for the regime you are now in, and the literature is unambiguous about it.** KataGo's gate is ≥100/200 wins — a *50% regression-protection bar*, officially optional ("possible to train just accepting every new model") [L: Wu arXiv:1902.10565 App. E; KataGo SelfplayTraining.md]. Lc0's promotion gate was *promote unless worse than −50 Elo*, later −150 [L: lczero.org project history]. Leela Zero's 55%/400 gate — the only comparable project that gated like you do — produced documented week-long promotion dry spells, and community analysis estimated ~7.6 Elo/net gained at the 55% bar vs ~12 at 50% [L: leela-zero issues #1091, #1524, #2425]. Your elo1=+30 bar at a true +20 candidate is Leela Zero's documented mistake, replayed. **Switch the flywheel gate to regression protection (elo0=−10, elo1=+10..15) with the external panel as the binding tripwire.** Details and safeguards in §5.

2. **Your c_scale=0.03 fix and your plateau are the same object.** The min-max rescale forces the completed-Q term to fill a fixed ~1.6–2.4-nat logit budget at every root regardless of how informative search was. Early on, true Q spread >> value noise, so that budget was signal: +49 Elo/turn. As the policy sharpens, true Q spread shrinks toward the (constant) value-noise floor, so a growing fraction of the same fixed budget is noise — the improvement operator's SNR decays *by construction*, predicting exactly your +49 → +49 → +33 → +20 compression at constant data volume. [J, with measurements proposed in §6 that would confirm or kill this.] The fix is not more training on the same window — it is **reducing root value noise (you already measured the 3.3× symmetry-averaging denoise and then didn't deploy it) and then re-tuning c_scale upward**, because the cs=0.03 optimum was measured at *current* noise.

3. **Your value-fragility "law" is real but misdiagnosed, and the field has three standard fixes you haven't tried.** The literature does not support "the value head cannot revisit a distribution." It supports: *MSE regression onto partially self-referential targets degrades under reuse* — mechanistically explained as effective-rank collapse that provably vanishes with pure Monte-Carlo outcome targets [L: Kumar et al. arXiv:2010.14498]. Your λ=0.5 blend makes half the value target self-referential; your four failures all involve reuse + that self-reference + MSE. Standard fixes, all cheaper than your avoidance regime: (a) **HL-Gauss categorical value head** — cross-entropy value training is specifically robust to noisy targets and non-stationarity, and *MSE degrades with prolonged training while CE does not* [L: Farebrother et al. arXiv:2403.03950, ICML 2024 oral]; note plain two-hot *underperforms* — if your shelved head is two-hot, upgrade it to HL-Gauss before testing; (b) **Reanalyze/ReZero target refresh** — reuse states, regenerate targets with the current net [L: Schrittwieser arXiv:2104.06294; ReZero arXiv:2404.16364]; (c) a **lagged target network** for the bootstrapped half of the value target [L: MuZero arXiv:1911.08265].

4. **The single most actionable paper for your plateau is RGSC (Regret-Guided Search Control), ICLR 2026** [L: Tsai et al. arXiv:2602.20809, github.com/rlglab/rgsc]: regret-prioritized restart-state selection **continued improving a nearly-converged 9×9 Go model from 69.3% → 78.2% where both vanilla AlphaZero and Go-Exploit flatlined** (+77/+89 Elo average over three games). You already built Go-Exploit-style restart tooling (extraction + bit-exact reconstruction) and never wired it into production data. Finish it, with RGSC's ranking-based regret prioritization instead of uniform sampling.

5. **Your opponent pool being "designed but not wired" is the inbreeding risk, not a nice-to-have.** Self-play against only the latest champion provably cycles in nontransitive regions [L: Balduzzi arXiv:1901.08106]; superhuman KataGo retained catastrophic blind spots invisible to self-play, and its actual defense was an 18%-frozen-past-opponent mixture [L: Wang et al. arXiv:2211.00241; Tseng et al. arXiv:2406.12843]; AlphaStar's main agents played only ~35% pure self-play [L: Vinyals et al., Nature 2019]. A 2026 result shows "competitive overfitting" where self-play winrate holds at ~50% while generalization collapses 73.5%→21.6%, *undetectable internally*; the fix was 20% of episodes vs a fixed non-adaptive opponent [L: "Territory Paint Wars," 2604.04983]. Wire the pool: start with 15–25% vs sampled past champions + a small % vs catanatron_value itself as a data opponent.

6. **Statistical overclaims to fix before anyone external reads the report.** (a) Gen-3 vs catanatron_value 45.7%/200: z = −1.22 vs 50%, p ≈ 0.22 — "still leads us by ~30 Elo" is a point estimate; parity is not excluded, and neither is a real deficit. You may already be at parity with your north star and don't know it. (b) The pooled turn-4 "52.8%, p ≈ 0.04" is one-sided (two-sided p = 0.08), pools two different measurement instruments (n=8 and n=16 gates), and drops the pairing structure. (c) Calling λ=0.5 "the single most validated training-science result": 59.0% vs the 57.0% control is a 2-point difference with ~±3.5-point SE on the difference — *the λ effect vs control was never established* (z ≈ 0.57), and it was the max of 7 arms (winner's curse). Adopting it was reasonable; the label is not. (d) The report's Elo arithmetic, CIs, and the pentanomial-SPRT design itself all check out — the gate *machinery* is textbook fishtest and correct [L: fishtest/GSPRT docs].

7. **First-mover claims need three corrections.** (a) "No reference implementation combines Gumbel with Stochastic-MuZero-style chance nodes" is **false**: Kao, Guei, Wu & Wu, "Gumbel MuZero for the Game of 2048" (IEEE TAAI 2022, doi:10.1109/taai57707.2022.00017) did exactly that combination — it is even linked from mctx issue #66. Your defensible claim is narrower: *perfect-simulator enumerated chance nodes + hidden-information masking + paired statistical gating in a 2-player board game*. (b) "No published Catan agent beats catanatron's bots" needs a qualifier: HEXMACHINA (anonymous ICLR 2026 submission, unreviewed) claims 54.1% vs AlphaBeta depth-2 via LLM-evolved heuristic code — not an RL/search-trained agent, so say "no published *learned* agent." (c) "CatAnalysis" appears not to exist under that name; the closest real prior is Driss & Cazenave, "Deep Catan" (ACG 2021) — 4-player expert iteration, benchmarked only against its own weak in-house UCT. Also: the "AB5 is weakest" catanatron quirk could not be verified in any catanatron source — re-derive it from your own data or drop it. Your *mctx-rescale pathology* novelty claim, by contrast, **survives scrutiny**: nothing in the literature or the mctx tracker documents search-loses-to-its-own-raw-policy via min-max-rescale noise amplification, and the Gumbel paper's own Atari setting (c_scale=0.1, 10× below Go/chess) is precedent for your direction but never went to 0.03 or isolated the rescale operator [L: mctx qtransforms.py; Danihelka et al. 2022; open mctx issue #108 asks your exact question, unanswered].

8. **You are sitting on a denoising lever your own economics make nearly free, and it unblocks everything else.** 12-fold D6 symmetry averaging gives 3.3× value-noise reduction (your measurement). The NN forward is 4% of leaf cost; a batched 12-symmetry forward is ≈ free on the GPU. The blocker is featurizing 12 orientations — which the staged Rust featurizer (20–38×) reduces to ~+12–18% leaf cost, or ~0% if you implement the D6 transform as a token-permutation + feature-index remap on the already-featurized tensor [J]. KataGo ships exactly this as `rootNumSymmetriesToSample=4` [L: KataGo docs]. Sequencing: land Rust featurize → turn on root symmetry averaging in generation and gates → re-run the c_scale grid (expect the optimum to move up) → re-test D1/D2, which were "mildly ahead" at old noise levels.

9. **Reanalyze your 32.6M-row banked corpus instead of treating old data as radioactive.** You have 417 GB of positions whose *states* are perfectly good and whose *targets* are stale. MuZero Reanalyse regenerates value/policy targets on stored trajectories with the current net — "converting revisiting data from failure mode into feature" [L: arXiv:2104.06294; ReZero arXiv:2404.16364 makes it cheap via periodic whole-buffer refresh]. At your leaf costs, value-only reanalyze (one forward per state, no search) is almost free; policy reanalyze on the 7.7% of full-search rows is a fleet-scale job but multiplies your effective data engine. This is the principled escape from "the window is fully distilled" that doesn't require promotion at all.

10. **Build the two evaluation instruments you're missing before you trust the next big decision.** (a) A **population round-robin** (all champions v3a→gen-4 + catanatron bots + raw policies, Nash-averaged or BayesElo) — internal pairwise ladders are exactly the structure Elo inflation exploits [L: Balduzzi arXiv:1806.02643], and a payoff matrix makes cycling/inbreeding *visible* (non-transitivity) instead of inferred. Cost: a few hundred games per pair at n=8, a weekend of one A100. (b) An **exploitability probe**: train a small adversary net via self-play against the *frozen* champion (the Wang et al. arXiv:2211.00241 methodology, which found >97% exploits vs superhuman KataGo at <14% of its compute). This is the only instrument that answers "is masked-AZ leaving a strategic hole" — AlphaZe** showed masked-AZ is a strong-but-bounded baseline below belief methods in Stratego [L: Blüml et al., Frontiers AI 2023], and whether that ceiling binds in Catan is unknowable from a fixed bot panel.

## 1. What you're doing wrong

### 1.1 The gate is spending your compounding
Expert iteration's entire mechanism is *generating the next corpus with a better policy*. A +20-Elo candidate promoted today improves every game generated from tomorrow; a +20 candidate held costs you the compounding forever. Your own ladder shows what compounding at ~+50/turn did in three days. The asymmetry of errors is stark [J]:

- **False promote** (true ≤ 0 Elo): detected within one round by a regression gate + external panel; cost ≈ one flywheel round.
- **False hold** (true +20): the flywheel generates gen-3-distribution data indefinitely; anchor telemetry is already flat; cost ≈ unbounded.

Leela Zero lived this exact failure (see §5). You designed the gate when turns were worth +50; the turns are now worth +20 and the gate cannot pass them — by design, at a true +20 and elo1=30, the SPRT walks to the cap and returns "continue" essentially always (your own LLR trajectories show it: +1.17 → +0.50 → −0.50).

### 1.2 The value-target design contains its own poison
`value_target = 0.5·z + 0.5·v_root` where `v_root` is backed up from the same net's value head. In the discrete regime, one dose per generation kept this safe. In the continuous flywheel, that term is a positive-feedback loop: the value head is partially trained toward its own (search-filtered) opinion, and your §11.3 drift pathology occurred *with λ=0.5 on*. Kumar et al. (arXiv:2010.14498) is almost a controlled experiment for your situation: bootstrapped/self-referential targets cause effective-rank collapse that compounds with data reuse, and the effect **vanishes with pure MC outcome targets**. Additionally, at plateau, `v_root ≈ v_net`, so the target degenerates toward `0.5·z + 0.5·v_net` — value learning slows exactly when you need fresh value signal [J].

Actions: anneal λ toward z as the flywheel matures, or replace the blend's bootstrap half with a *lagged* (EMA) value net's root value [L: MuZero's target network], or make revisits safe via Reanalyze so the bootstrap term is always the *current* net's fresh search (not a stored stale scalar). Note also that MuZero-Reanalyze *down-weighted value loss to 0.25 specifically as the sample-reuse countermeasure* [L: arXiv:1911.08265 appendix] — your value weight 1.0 + λ blend + reuse target 3.0 is a hotter configuration than the closest precedent ran.

### 1.3 Avoidance instead of structure
One-dose training, champion-init, anchor tripwires — all correct, all expensive, all treating the symptom. You built a categorical value head and never tested it, while the strongest relevant result of 2024 (ICML oral) says the cross-entropy value loss specifically removes the degradation-under-continued-training failure mode you keep hitting, with the biggest gains precisely under stochastic dynamics [L: Farebrother et al. 2403.03950 — and their sticky-actions ablation shows CE ≈ MSE under *deterministic* dynamics but CE >> MSE under *stochastic*; Catan is maximally stochastic]. Caveat that matters: their two-hot variant *underperformed MSE*; HL-Gauss (Gaussian label smoothing over bins) was the winner, and your bounded [−1,1] target dodges HL-Gauss's one known weakness (support-range selection). If your built head is two-hot, convert before testing. Your auxiliary heads carried at zero weight are the same pattern: KataGo attributes a large share of its speedup to ownership/score auxiliary targets regularizing the trunk [L: Wu 2019] — you built the Catan analogs and forfeit the benefit entirely.

### 1.4 Diversity is being strangled from three directions at once
Deterministic search + seed-derived chance, argmax after decision 90, and 100% champion-vs-champion data. Each alone is defensible; together they shrink the support of the self-play distribution every generation as the policy sharpens [J]. AlphaZero's own paper measured the cost of low diversity: forcing near-tie softmax sampling raised its win rate vs Stockfish from 5.8% to 14% in the low-diversity match regime [L: Science 2018 supplement]. KataGo's countermeasures are all cheap and shipped: root policy temperature 1.1–1.25 early-game (explicitly designed for near-tied moves), policy-surprise weighting (oversample states where search disagreed with the prior — half of KataGo's training-sample frequency weight), forced playouts + policy-target pruning at the root [L: KataGoMethods.md]. Your temperature window (90 of ~200 decisions) is proportionally ~3× AlphaZero's 30-of-~250 — but temperature applies only to played-action sampling while your *search itself* is deterministic, so identical seeds still produce identical trees; the diversity measurements in §6 will tell you whether this is actually biting.

### 1.5 Measurement blind spots
- **The n=8/n=16 gate vs n=64 production gap**: only gen-1 was ever confirmed at production budget. At ±20-Elo margins the low-sim/high-sim ordering can invert (your own §8.3 logic says low-sim gates amplify prior differences — that also means they can *over-credit* prior-only improvements that wash out under production search). Any relaxed promotion rule must include a periodic n=64 confirmation.
- **The anchor holdout does not predict gates** — gen-4 showed "the historical promotion signature" and gated flat. Your own Gate-A lesson ("only strength H2H binds") applies to the anchor too. Treat anchor telemetry as a drift tripwire, never as a promotion signal.
- **Bucketed win rates before promoting**: an aggregate +20 can hide a sub-population regression (opening-type, game-phase, opponent-archetype). This is cheap due diligence on games you already play [L: the "flip-centered gating" argument, AgentDevel 2601.04620; lc0's documented RPS-gating episode via fixed openings, leela-zero #2146].

### 1.6 Report hygiene (fix before external review)
The statistical items in Exec-Summary #6; the landscape corrections in #7; "Willemsen et al. (NCA 2021)" is ALA-2020-workshop/NCA-2022; and the exact-SH negative result has an unexamined confound — Karnin-style SH formally *discards statistics between rounds* while mctx *stockpiles* visits, and the bandit literature reports stockpiling dominates empirically [L: Cazenave, "Sequential Halving Using Scores"]. If your "exact-budget SH" port was restart-style, the 45.9% loss conflates budget accounting with statistics-discarding. Check the implementation before you publish that claim.

## 2. The plateau: unified diagnosis

Three mutually compatible mechanisms, in my order of likely contribution [J]:

**(A) Improvement-operator SNR decay (search side).** The mechanism in Exec-Summary #2. The min-max rescale guarantees the completed-Q term has constant magnitude; only its *signal fraction* changes. Signal = true Q spread among top candidates (shrinking as the policy converges toward search's fixed point); noise = 1–2-sample value noise (constant, and your D6-symmetry measurement says much of it is orientation noise — 0.175 nats vs the 0.06-nat true spread at openings). When signal/noise → 1, π' ≈ prior + noise, and distillation has nothing to learn. This predicts compression at *constant* data volume, which is what you observe; "window fully distilled" is the training-side shadow of this search-side fact.

**(B) Expert-iteration fixed point (theory).** ExIt's learning rate is governed by the apprentice–expert gap, which shrinks as the apprentice approaches what the *fixed-budget* expert can produce [L: Anthony et al. arXiv:1705.08439; Huszár's fixed-point framing]. ELF OpenGo measured the complementary ceiling: even final models gained ~200 Elo per rollout-doubling — the fixed point moves outward with search budget [L: arXiv:1902.04522]. Compression is the expected trajectory; escaping it requires moving the fixed point (budget, capacity, data distribution), not iterating harder at the same configuration.

**(C) Distribution narrowing / inbreeding (data side).** §1.4. The external panel flatness at the last internal +20 is the one piece of evidence specifically pointing here — internal gains that don't transfer are the signature of exploiting the previous champion's style [L: Balduzzi 1806.02643; Territory Paint Wars].

Discriminating measurements in §6. Note these mechanisms have *different* remedies — (A) wants denoising + c_scale retune + more sims, (B) wants budget/capacity, (C) wants opponent diversity — which is why diagnosing before spending the next two weeks matters.

## 3. How I would do it (deltas only)

- **Gate**: regression-protection SPRT (elo0=−10, elo1=+10) at n=16, 300+ext games, for *flywheel* promotion; every 3rd promotion also needs (i) a 200-game n=64 non-regression check and (ii) no external-panel decline on the 2-of-3-bot majority. Revert rule: two consecutive external declines → roll back fleet to prior champion (cheap; you have the registry). This is KataGo's bar plus your external tripwire, and it converts the flywheel from "blocked" to "compounding with a safety net."
- **Value head**: HL-Gauss categorical (31–51 bins on [−1,1], σ ≈ bin width), value-loss weight down to 0.25–0.5 in the flywheel (MuZero-Reanalyze precedent), λ blend annealed or lag-netted. Auxiliary ownership-analog heads (final settlements/cities per node, final VP margin) at weight 0.02–0.1.
- **Search**: root symmetry averaging (after Rust featurize lands), then re-run the {c_scale × D1} grid; forced playouts + policy-target pruning at wide roots; keep lazy interior chance; raise n_full 64→96/128 *after* denoising (your leaf economics now permit it — that's the (B) lever), gated on D4 calibration per MiniZero's progressive-simulation logic [L: arXiv:2310.11305].
- **Data engine**: opponent pool 20% (past champions, PFSP-hardest weighting) + 5% vs catanatron_value; RGSC-style regret restarts for 10–20% of games (tooling exists); policy-surprise weighting in the loader; value-only Reanalyze pass over the banked corpus each generation.
- **Architecture**: hold at 35M until the data engine improves (your v3a-vs-v3b A/B supports this), then scale to ~90M with a fresh-data plan sized by Neumann & Gros — bigger nets need *proportionally more fresh data*, and their NeurIPS 2025 follow-up warns of inverse scaling when end-game states are over-represented — check your phase distribution first [L: arXiv:2210.00849, arXiv:2412.11979]. When you do scale, the VISA trick (symmetry variants chosen for *maximum value disagreement* as extra value-training points) plugs directly into your D6 finding [L: Grupen et al. arXiv:2301.11857 — 50% value-generalization-error reduction].
- **Evaluation**: population round-robin + Nash averaging; exploitability probe (§7 experiment 9); 1000-game external panels for any claim you put in writing.

## 5. Q1 — Promotion criterion (the live decision)

**Recommendation: option 1, hardened.** Concretely:

1. Flywheel gate → elo0=−10, elo1=+10, α=β=0.05, n=16, 300 games + extensions to 600. At a true +20 this passes quickly; at a true −5 it blocks reliably. (Also defensible per the SPRT literature: size elo1 to the *achievable* effect — fishtest runs elo1 of 2–5 for exactly this reason.)
2. **External panel becomes binding**: 400 games vs catanatron_value + AB3 per promotion (you generate ~this volume in gate extensions today); two consecutive declines on the majority of the panel → automatic fleet rollback. This is your anti-inbreeding tripwire, and it directly addresses the one observation that argues for holding (external flatness).
3. Every 3rd promotion: 200-game n=64 non-regression confirmation (closes the n=8/16-vs-production gap).
4. Track the population payoff matrix (all champions + bots); promotion decisions consult Nash-averaged rating trend, not just the pairwise gate [L: Balduzzi 1806.02643].
5. Bucket the gate games (phase/opening/blowout-vs-close) and refuse promotion on any bucket regressing > some threshold even if aggregate passes.

The counterargument (external flatness ⇒ maybe inbreeding) is better answered by instruments (#2, #4, the exploitability probe) than by freezing the flywheel: a hold produces *zero* information while burning fleet-hours on a fully-distilled distribution. What comparable projects did: KataGo promoted at 50%; AlphaZero/MuZero promoted always; Lc0 promoted unless clearly worse; the only project with your bar (LZ) documented the stall and the community's post-mortem recommended exactly this loosening. "Survive or Collapse" (2605.22217) adds the modern caveat: keep *a* gate — data admission gating is the binding stability constraint — which is why I recommend −10, not ungated.

## 6. Q2/Q3 — Escaping the plateau, and diagnosing the compression

**Diagnostics first (cheap, this week) [E]:**
- **Search-SNR probe**: at ~200 sampled full-search roots per generation checkpoint (v3a…gen-4), run the same search twice with different search seeds; report argmax-agreement and KL(π'₁‖π'₂), plus KL(π'‖prior). Prediction under mechanism (A): KL(π'‖prior) roughly constant across generations (rescale forces it) while cross-seed agreement decays toward the noise floor. If instead agreement is stable and KL(π'‖prior) is shrinking, (A) is wrong and the plateau is (B)/(C).
- **Rollout-doubling Elo** (ELF OpenGo diagnostic): champion at n=64 vs champion at n=128, 400 paired games. Big gap ⇒ net capacity is not the binding constraint, search budget is; tiny gap ⇒ capacity/data-side. Also check gain symmetry by seat (their asymmetric-ceiling lesson).
- **Diversity telemetry**: unique-state fraction, mean policy entropy at decisions 1–30, and opening-line concentration per generation. Falling curves ⇒ (C) is live.
- **Value-noise vs signal trend**: your D6 orientation-noise std vs top-5-candidate Q-spread, per generation. If spread → noise, (A) confirmed directly.

**Escape levers, ranked by (expected Elo)/(engineering cost) [J]:**
1. Promote under the §5 gate (free; fresh distribution immediately).
2. Wire opponent pool at 20% + 5% vs catanatron_value (small; addresses (C) and the external gap simultaneously).
3. Root symmetry averaging + c_scale/D1 re-grid (medium; addresses (A) at its root).
4. RGSC regret restarts (small-medium; tooling exists; the one lever with direct evidence of un-sticking a *converged* AZ).
5. Value-only Reanalyze over the banked corpus + HL-Gauss head (medium; multiplies data engine, removes the fragility tax).
6. n_full 64→96/128 after (3) (compute-priced by your own lazy-chance economics; addresses (B)).
7. LR-schedule experiment — you run constant 3e-5; KataGo's late-run history shows large discontinuous gains at LR drops [L: KataGo TrainingHistory.md]. One flywheel arm at 0.5× LR costs nothing to try.
8. 90M net with a fresh-data budget sized to it (last; blocked on data engine per your own 91M probe and Neumann & Gros).

## 7. Q4–Q8 — remaining answers

**Q4 (external gap).** Internal>external inflation is normal and formalized (Nash-averaging paper); your ×2 ratio is not alarming by itself. What *is* missing: (a) power — your panels can't resolve the differences you're interpreting (the 1000-game panel fixes this; keep it as the standard for any written claim); (b) a population payoff matrix (non-transitivity = inbreeding made visible); (c) the exploitability probe — train an adversary vs the frozen champion (Wang et al. methodology). That last one is the strongest external-validity instrument in the 2023–2026 literature and doubles as the masked-AZ ceiling test (Q5): if a cheap adversary finds a >70% exploit, your masking/equilibrium story has a hole; if not, masked-AZ is fine for this game. My prior: Catan 2p no-trade hidden info is mostly fog, not bluffing — the Perolat non-convergence mode probably doesn't bind — but this is exactly the kind of prior worth 3 GPU-days to test [E].

**Q5 (value fragility).** Answered in §1.2–1.3. One-line version: it's not "can't revisit," it's "MSE + self-referential targets + reuse"; fixes are HL-Gauss, Reanalyze, lagged targets, value-weight 0.25, aux heads on — in that order of evidence strength.

**Q6 (wide stochastic roots).** Your cs=0.03 result stands and now has a mechanism, a precedent (Atari 0.1), and an open upstream question it answers (mctx #108). The literature adds: KataGo's uncertainty-weighted playouts *required a weight cap* to work — re-test D2 with a cap; Epistemic MCTS (ICLR 2025) is the principled uncertainty-propagation reference; forced playouts + policy-target pruning is the shipped mechanism most directly aimed at near-tied wide roots; and the James-Stein shrinkage coefficient has a closed form you can set from measured within-arm vs across-arm variance instead of hand-tuning (λ* = v²/(s²+v²) form). The right sequence is denoise → re-tune, not more operator engineering at current noise.

**Q7 (architecture).** The v3b A/B was one seed, one data scale, and v3b was only 36% bigger — the honest read is "architecture doesn't matter *yet at this data scale*," not "cross-attention is bad" [J]. Don't invest until the data engine improves; when you do, priority is (1) scale with fresh data, (2) resurrect v3b's action-cross-attention *at* scale, (3) symmetry as training-time augmentation + VISA hard negatives rather than architectural D6-equivariance (AlphaZero itself dropped symmetry exploitation and won; equivariance for hex transformers has no precedent and your token-permutation trick captures most of the value for none of the risk).

**Q8 (what you're not asking).** Four items, first two load-bearing:
1. **You may already have beaten your north star and can't see it** — 45.7% ± 7 vs catanatron_value is statistically compatible with parity. If the 1000-game panel lands at ~48–52%, the headline goal flips from "close the −30 gap" to "prove superiority with power," which changes what you build next (power > levers). Relatedly, your entire external ladder runs inside your own Rust engine's cross-play harness; catanatron's own tiny-sample ladder (AB2 beats VF 80% over n=25 in *their* harness) doesn't even agree with your ordering of VF > AB3 — before claiming #1, run the definitive matches in *their* engine (or a neutral referee) so no one can attribute the result to engine bias [E].
2. **"#1 Catan bot" is currently scoped to 2-player no-trade**, and the trade literature says negotiation alone moves win rates by double digits (and is the one place a Catan AI ever beat humans). Nobody has ever measured how a no-trade champion transfers to the full game. The claim you can actually defend in 2026 is "strongest 2p no-trade agent, first leak-free gated AZ-class Catan system." Decide *now* whether 4p+trade is in scope for the goal, because the architecture choices (action space, negotiation, multi-agent value) are much cheaper to plan for than to retrofit.
3. **Nobody on the outside can reproduce anything** — the H2H gate tooling is untracked in git on one host (your own audit says so), and your strongest scientific claims (rescale pathology, exact-SH result) have publishable value only with clean repro. Two days of repo hygiene buys the credibility your first-mover claims need.
4. **A human benchmark doesn't exist and won't build itself.** No agreed superhuman-Catan yardstick exists (Colonist.io ladder is human-only). If superhuman is the endgame, start the bridge early: a Colonist-compatible client or a small invited human match series against strong ranked players, with the same pentanomial rigor you already have.
