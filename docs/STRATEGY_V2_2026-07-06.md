## Why redo the strategy
Two flywheel turns each gained +57% (SPRT H1) — but every attempt to extract *more* from a turn by re-training on its frozen corpus failed with one mechanism (value-head memorization: 16k outcome bits/generation vs 35M params), and 16 search-config challengers all lost to production. Meanwhile 10k-game and 12k-game generations produced *identical* gains, suggesting per-turn improvement is capped by the search operator, not corpus size. The literature agrees on all three fronts (see Sources).

## The five moves

### 1. Gen-3 is the LAST discrete generation → flip continuous
Evidence: KataGo/OpenSpiel production designs are continuous; a fixed-budget ablation (PSV-VERUS) found more-frequent-smaller updates beat fewer-bigger ones; SPAG found sharp diminishing returns past a modest game count; our own 10k≈12k result. ARM-C (recipe-A on a random half of the gen-2 corpus, gate seed 91M) empirically tests the corpus-size slope tonight.
**Plan:** train gen-3 on the incoming ~16.5k-game corpus with the matrix-winning recipe → gate → promote → **flip to `tools/continuous_flywheel.py`** (built, fixed, committed at ea6ce93).

### 2. Continuous-mode operating parameters
- **GPU split ~8:1 generation:training** (KataGo doc guidance is 4-40x; start: all 40 L4s + ~12 A100s generating, 2 B200s training, 2 A100s gating; retune reactively — trainer idling → shift to train; buffer backlog → shift to gen).
- **Cold-start floor:** no training until the window holds ≥ ~3M rows (≈ one discrete generation — KataGo's MIN_ROWS concept scaled to us).
- **Opponent pool ON** (15-20%, archive = {v3a, gen-1, gen-2A}; wired end-to-end, provenance-tagged; anti-forgetting + decorrelated value outcomes).
- **Gate cadence:** cheap masked h2h gate per candidate (~500k new rows), promote on SPRT accept only.
- **Anti-blind-spot rule** (arXiv:2604.04983 — internal gates can stay "healthy" while true strength collapses): every promoted champion ALSO plays a fixed external panel — catanatron_value + AB3 on the TOURNAMENT-map bridge — and we track that trend as the true north star. catanatron_value is the one bot that beats us; it becomes the leading indicator.

### 3. Scale the net — aggressive, parallel, isolated credit
Neumann & Gros (arXiv:2210.00849): strength ∝ params^0.88; published game nets are *systematically smaller than compute-optimal*. Our unique economics: NN forward = ~4% of leaf cost (featurize+FFI dominate), so a 2-3x net is nearly free at generation time — an advantage no published AZ project had.
**Plan:** train a ~70-100M entity_graph net DIRECTLY on the accumulated window (no net2net — zero literature precedent; KataGo/lc0 both trained bigger nets straight on the buffer). Run as one clean A/B (only net size changes) inside continuous mode. Note lc0/KataGo's later lesson: past a point, *architecture* beat raw scale — the action-cross-attention/value-pooling branch is the follow-on.

### 4. Search-budget curriculum — gated, not eager
MiniZero: raising sims *before* the net is reliable actively hurts (weaker than fixed-budget until ~270 iterations), but crossing budget thresholds when ready produced a +276 Elo jump. Danihelka: n≤16 breaks learning outright (floor confirmed).
**Plan:** gen-3 stays n_full=64. Bump to 96 only when D4 phase-sliced calibration passes on the current champion; 128 only if calibration keeps improving AND the search-vs-raw margin isn't shrinking. **New telemetry: root KL(prior ‖ improved policy)** per generation (prior_policy is already in shards) — shrinking KL = search no longer improving the prior at this budget = the plateau signature (arXiv:2306.00840) and the trigger to raise n_full or grow the net.

### 5. The value program (unchanged, now the main experimental surface)
- Hybrid value targets from `target_scores` (λ-blend; λ=1.0 = Willemsen soft-Z, 2-3x faster convergence in their games) — implementation in progress, arms H1/H1b/H2 gate tonight.
- Train-time 12-fold symmetry (id-numbering verified bit-exact).
- Go-Exploit restarts (#64) → then **Regret-Guided Search Control** (arXiv:2602.20809, the ICLR-26 successor: restart from high-regret states ranked by a learned network) as the increment after.
- Categorical value head (f76) as a gen-4 candidate; multi-horizon KataGo aux targets pending the #63 audit.

## Kill list (closed questions — do not refund)
Multi-epoch reuse of frozen corpora (3 experiments, 1 mechanism); c_visit/c_scale tuning (16 challengers, 0 winners; cv25 final 50.1% H0); fixed-bounds qtransform (H0); optimizer choice at 1 epoch (identical); pure-CPU Modal generation (economics); label smoothing on z (no precedent, no mechanism).

## Success metrics (in order)
1. External panel trend (catanatron_value first, AB3/AB4) — true strength.
2. Champion promotion rate per day in continuous mode (target ≥2 promotions/day early).
3. Root-KL and D4 calibration trends — leading indicators.
4. Gate margins vs previous champion — lagging confirmation, never sole evidence (blind-spot rule).
